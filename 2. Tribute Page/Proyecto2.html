Basic Information:

Osamu Dazai was a Japanese author born in 1909 in Kanagi, Akita prefecture. He is known for his novels and short stories that often explore themes of isolation and despair. Dazai attended Waseda University in Tokyo, where he studied French literature and became a member of the literary club. He is best known for his novel "No Longer Human", which was published in 1948 and remains one of the most popular and influential works of Japanese literature. Dazai struggled with depression and alcoholism throughout his life and died by suicide in 1950 at the age of 40.

  • 1936-1940: Dazai continues to write and publish fiction, including his first novel, "School of Flesh," which is released in 1938. He also gets married and has a daughter during this time.
